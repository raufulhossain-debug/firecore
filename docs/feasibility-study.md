# Wildfire-Resilient ADU — 20-Parcel Feasibility Study
**City of Los Angeles | April 2026**

---

## Purpose

Pressure-test a single productized SKU — a **1,200 sqft wildfire-resilient ADU** — against 20 representative Los Angeles parcels to answer one question:

> *Does a single certified SKU, with a bounded envelope of parametric variation, fit enough real parcels to justify building the licensing/configurator business around it?*

If ≥70% of parcels can accept the SKU (exact-fit or minor-variation), the product concept is viable. If ≤40%, the variation space is too wide and the catalog would have to grow faster than is economic.

---

## The SKU under test: "FireCore-1200"

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Type | Detached ADU |
| Conditioned area | 1,200 sqft (fixed) |
| Footprint options | 30×40 ft (single-story) OR 24×25 ft (two-story) |
| Height | 16 ft (1-story) / 25 ft (2-story) |
| Orientation | Rotatable in 15° increments |
| Foundation | Slab-on-grade OR raised pier (flood-capable) |
| Cladding | Class-A fiber cement, ember-resistant vents, tempered dual-pane glazing, 1-hr rated eaves |
| Compliance target | CBC Chapter 7A (WUI), Title 24 2022, CalGreen Tier 1 |
| Certification path | LA City pre-approved ADU program (Standard Plan Program) |

**Parametric envelope** (variation allowed without voiding certification):
- Rotation: any 15° increment
- Window placement: within 12 pre-defined glazing slots
- Entry location: 4 pre-defined options
- Cladding color: 6 certified finishes
- Roof pitch: 3:12, 4:12, or 5:12 (all Class-A rated)

Anything outside this envelope requires re-stamp — which defeats the product model.

## LA City ADU rules applied

Drawn from **LAMC Section 12.22 A.33** and state ADU law (Gov. Code §65852.2):

- **Minimum lot size:** none (state preemption)
- **Maximum ADU size:** 1,200 sqft (detached) — matches SKU exactly
- **Setbacks:** 4 ft side/rear (state minimum); front per underlying zone
- **Height:** up to 16 ft by-right; up to 25 ft if within ½ mi of major transit (2-story variant applicable)
- **Lot coverage:** must preserve underlying zone's open-space minimums
- **Parking:** waived if within ½ mi of transit
- **Fire:** Chapter 7A materials required in VHFHSZ; defensible space per PRC §4291 (100 ft)
- **Flood:** if in SFHA (FEMA Zone A/AE/VE), lowest floor must be ≥ BFE + LA freeboard (1 ft); raised-pier variant applies

## Fit evaluation

Each parcel is scored against FireCore-1200 on six gates:

1. **Lot size adequate** — can buildable envelope after setbacks accommodate 30×40 or 24×25 footprint?
2. **Coverage headroom** — does adding ~1,000 sqft footprint keep lot coverage under zone max (~45–50% typical)?
3. **Height allowed** — is 2-story variant permitted (requires ½-mi transit OR qualifying lot)?
4. **Fire compliance path** — is SKU's Chapter 7A package sufficient, or does site demand enhanced measures (Class-A roof alone vs. full 7A vs. fire-sprinklered)?
5. **Flood compliance path** — does raised-pier variant + SKU freeboard handle the flood zone?
6. **Slope feasibility** — is slope ≤15% (SKU designed for gentle-to-moderate) or does it need a custom foundation?

**Outcome categories:**
- **EXACT FIT** — all six gates pass with 1-story SKU, no variation required
- **MINOR VARIATION** — fits via in-envelope choices (rotation, 2-story, raised pier, roof pitch)
- **MAJOR VARIATION** — would fit with engineering changes outside certified envelope (custom foundation, reduced footprint)
- **NO FIT** — cannot accept this SKU

## Interpretation

**The headline number is good.** 80% of a realistic LA parcel mix can accept FireCore-1200 without breaking certification — which passes my ≥70% viability threshold. A single SKU with a modest envelope of parametric variation covers a large majority of the LA ADU market.

**The failure mode is consistent: slope.** Every MAJOR-variation parcel (4 of 4) failed on steep slope, not on fire, flood, zoning, or coverage. That's a crisp product insight:

- Slope is the **single highest-value expansion** of the certified envelope. Adding a certified "stepped pier" foundation variant would likely move 3 of those 4 parcels (Mandeville, Silver Lake, Encino) into MINOR, pushing in-envelope fit to ~95%.
- The one remaining hold-out (Woodland Hills hillside, combined steep + VHFHSZ) is a candidate for a second SKU — a true hillside WUI product — not an envelope expansion.

**Fire compliance was not a binding constraint.** 7 of 20 parcels are in VHFHSZ or Moderate FHSZ, and FireCore-1200's native Chapter 7A package handled all of them. This confirms the SKU thesis: *certify hard once, deploy broadly*.

**Flood compliance was not a binding constraint either** (2 of 20 in SFHA, both handled by raised-pier variant). Flood is a smaller LA submarket but the variant cost is modest.

**Coverage tightness in dense zones** (Koreatown, Mid-City) pushes buyers to the 2-story variant. That's fine — it's in envelope — but it tells you the 2-story version isn't optional; it's required to cover the inner-city ADU market.

---

## What this study does *not* prove

- It does not prove buyer demand. It proves *technical fit*. Separate question: at what royalty do licensees (modular factories) pay?
- It does not reflect assessor-roll distribution. Real LA has far more R1-flat parcels than RE-hillside, so real-world fit rates may be *higher* than 80%.
- It does not include neighbor-facing constraints (HPOZ historic overlays, coastal zone ≤ CA Coastal Commission, hillside ordinance). Roughly ~15% of LA parcels carry one of these overlays; a production rules engine must model them.
- It does not test the permit process. Fit ≠ approved. LA Building & Safety still reviews each submittal against the Standard Plan Program. Typical review is 4–8 weeks even for pre-approved plans.

---

## Recommended next moves

1. **Expand the certified envelope to include a stepped-pier hillside variant** before launch. This single addition moves projected fit from 80% to ~95% and opens the entire LA hillside ADU market.
2. **File FireCore-1200 into LA's Standard Plan Program.** Approved plans skip planning review and go directly to building plan check. This is the real commercial wedge; the software layer is secondary.
3. **Add a second SKU for extreme hillside + VHFHSZ** only after the flat-lot version is licensed to 2+ factories and producing revenue.
4. **Mirror the rules engine to San Jose, San Diego, Oakland** — all three run their own Pre-Approved ADU programs, same structural template, different code details.

The product hypothesis survives contact with real LA geography. The slope gap is the first thing to design around.
